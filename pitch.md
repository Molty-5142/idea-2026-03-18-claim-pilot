# ClaimPilot — Investor / Co-Founder Pitch

---

## Problem
When homeowners file an insurance claim, their insurer uses AI-powered adjusters to minimize payouts — and homeowners have no AI of their own, leaving them to accept lowball offers or pay a public adjuster 10–15% of their settlement.

## Solution
ClaimPilot is an AI co-pilot that reads your insurance policy and claim estimate, identifies what's been underpaid or denied, and generates a professional supplement request letter — for a $99 flat fee, in minutes.

---

## Vision
Every homeowner deserves a world-class insurance advocate. ClaimPilot becomes the consumer-side AI layer in the claims process — the tool that rebalances an asymmetric information war. Long-term: real-time claim monitoring, multi-line insurance (auto, health), and agentic filing that contacts insurers directly on the homeowner's behalf.

---

## Market

- **5.7M** homeowner claims filed annually in the US
- **$130B+** annual homeowner insurance premiums
- **$3B** public adjuster industry — our direct comp
- Public adjusters charge 10–15% of final settlement. On a $15,749 average claim, that's $1,500–$2,250 per claim.
- ClaimPilot charges $99 flat. Same outcome. Better math for the homeowner.
- **TAM**: $500M+ (1% claims penetration at $99 = $5.6M ARR; 10% = $56M ARR)

---

## Why Now

1. **AI arms race is live**: Insurers deployed AI in Q4 2025 specifically to deny and minimize claims (Reuters, March 2026). Homeowners have no equivalent.
2. **Consumer frustration is peaking**: 54% of homeowners saw premium increases in 2025. Trust in insurers is at an all-time low. Reddit's r/Insurance is full of rage.
3. **LLMs can now read complex documents**: GPT-4 class models can parse policy language, Xactimate line items, and building codes with high accuracy. This wasn't possible 2 years ago.
4. **FairMedBill validated the category**: A 2025 startup raised on the same thesis — AI for bill disputes — in medical. Home insurance is next.
