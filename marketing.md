# marketing.md — ClaimPilot Go-To-Market

## Core Message
**"Your insurer has AI. Now you do too."**

---

## Out-of-the-Box Distribution Ideas

### 1. Reddit as Warm Lead Engine
- r/Insurance, r/HomeImprovement, r/personalfinance get 5–10 posts/week from frustrated homeowners post-denial
- Build a bot (or manual process initially) that monitors these threads
- Post a helpful comment: "Here's how you write a supplement request..." then organically mention ClaimPilot
- This community is already asking for exactly what you're building

### 2. Contractor Partnership Channel (Underrated)
- Roofing, plumbing, and restoration contractors get underpaid constantly on insurance jobs
- They act as informal claim advocates for their customers — but have no tools
- Partner with contractors: give them a white-labeled ClaimPilot link to share with homeowners
- Rev share or flat referral fee per claim
- A roofing company filing 50 storm claims/year is a $5K/year revenue partner

### 3. "Claim Review" TikTok / YouTube Series
- Create short-form content: "I got a $4,000 claim estimate. Watch me get it to $9,000 in 20 minutes"
- Real walkthroughs of using ClaimPilot to dispute actual claims
- This is high shareability content — every homeowner who sees it has had a claim or knows someone who has

### 4. Public Adjuster Ecosystem Play
- Public adjusters are overwhelmed with small claims (under $10K) — not worth their time at 10-15%
- Partner with PA firms: they refer small claims to ClaimPilot, keep their capacity for large claims
- Positions ClaimPilot as PA infrastructure, not a competitor

### 5. Disaster Response Surge Channel
- When a storm hits (hurricane, hail, wildfire), claims surge 10x in that region
- Build a Google Ads + Facebook Ads playbook that targets affected zip codes within 72 hours of a disaster
- "Did your home get hit by [Storm Name]? Here's how to get the full payout."
- This is time-sensitive demand — homeowners google this immediately

### 6. Insurance State Regulators / Consumer Advocacy Groups
- Many state insurance commissioners run "help" hotlines for underpaid claims
- Partner with consumer advocacy orgs: National Association of Public Insurance Adjusters (NAPIA), state insurance bureaus
- Get listed as a recommended consumer resource

### 7. "Fight Your Insurer" Newsletter
- Weekly email: here's what insurers denied this week, here's how to respond
- Pull from public claim dispute records and Reddit posts
- Build authority, drive inbound

### 8. Pre-Claim Onboarding (Preventative)
- Offer a free "policy audit" — upload your policy, ClaimPilot tells you coverage gaps and what to document if damage occurs
- This is a lead gen tool with massive WOM potential: "this AI told me I was underinsured"
- Converts free users to paid when they actually file a claim

---

## Pricing Approach
- **Free tier**: Upload policy + get a coverage summary
- **$99/claim**: Full audit + supplement draft letter
- **$249/claim**: Full audit + letter + 30-day follow-up assistance
- **Enterprise/Contractor**: $500/month for unlimited claims (for restoration companies)

## Launch Sequence
1. Soft launch in Reddit communities (organic)
2. Contractor outreach (personal calls, 10 partnerships)
3. Storm season Google/Meta ads playbook
4. TikTok content engine
5. PR: pitch as "the AI that fights back against your insurer"
